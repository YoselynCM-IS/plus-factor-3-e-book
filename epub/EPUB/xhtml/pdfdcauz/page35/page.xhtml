An
essay 
consists of  
three 
parts:

 

a .  
The introductory paragraph or introduction. 
It contains the topic sentence (a

 

sentence that states the main point of a paragraph). It tells the reader what the

 

essay will be about. It is usually the first sentence of a paragraph.

 

b .  
The body of the paragraph (supporting sentences). 
Here is where you include

 

specific details, reasons, examples or facts that support the topic sentence. If

 

the introductory paragraph contains two, three or more points to explain, each

 

body paragraph should support them.

 

c .  
The concluding sentence 
summarizes the key point of the thesis paragraph. It

 

connects the information discussed and the topic sentence. It is generally the

 

last sentence of the paragraph.
