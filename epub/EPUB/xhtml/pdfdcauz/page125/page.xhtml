DISCOURSE MARKERS

They are words or phrases we use to connect, organize, order or sequence our ideas.

We use different discourse markers in speaking and writing.

In speaking, the following discourse markers are very common:  
anyway, like, right, you know, fine,

now, so, I mean, good, oh, well, as I say, great, okay, mind you, for a start

In writing, the following discourse markers are common:  
firstly, secondly, thirdly, in addition,

moreover, in conclusion, in sum, on the one hand, on the other hand, to begin with

MODALS

ABILITY: 
Can / Be able to 
- express ability in the present or future.

Could 
- expresses general ability in the past.

Was / Were able to 
expresses ability in a specific situation in the past.

The negative of  
could / was (were) able to 
shows lack of ability in the past.

POSSIBILITY: 
We use  
may /might / could 
to express possibility in the present or future.

 
We use  
might 
to express small possibility or unreal situations.

 
May
not
and  
Might 
not
express lack of possibility in the present.
