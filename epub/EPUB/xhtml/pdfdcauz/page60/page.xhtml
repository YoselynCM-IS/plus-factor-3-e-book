Gerund and Infinitive

 

Some verbs can be followed by a -to infinitive.

 

These are some of the verbs which are usually followed by an infinitive.

 

afford, agree, appear, claim, decide, deserve, expect, help, hope, intend, invite, manage,

 

need, offer, plan, prefer, pretend, promise, refuse, seem, threaten, try, want, wish

 

Examples

 

I can’t 
afford to buy  
a new car.

 

They 
refused to tell  
the truth.

 

She
seems to be  
really interested in languages.

 

These are some of the verbs which are usually followed by a gerund (-ing).

 

admit, adore, avoid, can’t help, can’t stand, confess, deny, discuss, dislike, enjoy, finish, like,

 

keep, mind, miss, quit, recommend, regret, suggest

 

Examples

 

I
adore swimming  
in the sea.

 

The workers 
finished painting  
the house last Monday.

 

I
regret spending  
money on unnecessary things.
