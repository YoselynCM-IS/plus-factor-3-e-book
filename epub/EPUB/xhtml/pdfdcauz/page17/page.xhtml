Comparative and superlative form of adverbs

 

Adverbs ending in  
–ly
, use  
more 
to form the comparative. If we need to form the

 

superlative, we must use  
most 
.

 

kind  
more kindly  
most kindly

 

patient  
more patiently  
most patiently

 

Example:

 

I think you were quite rude to your uncle. You should apologize to him and talk to

 

him  
more kindly 
and  
more respectfully 
.

 

If an adverb does not end in 
–ly 
, use the same form as an adjective,  
-er
for

 

comparatives,  
-est 
for superlatives.

 

hard harder  
hardest

 

fast faster  
fastest

 

Example:

 

Arthur works  
harder than 
any of his workmates and got a prize for being the best

 

employee in the company.

 

Irregular adverbs have different forms.

 

well better  
best

 

badly worse  
worst

 

Example:

 

What happened to you? You played  
worse than 
last game. You know you can play

 

better than  
that, can’t you?
