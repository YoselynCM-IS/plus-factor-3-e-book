ave you ever thought about practicing a high risk sport? Well,

 

there are many options, for example: rock climbing, road

 

skateboarding, rafting or kite surfing. All of these activities are full of adrenaline and stress. Your body has to

 

be perfectly trained to resist that uncomfortable sensation of your muscles aching and getting hard until you

 

can’t move anymore. Physical strength is the main ingredient in these activities but it also involves the ability

 

to move not only carefully but efficiently so your physical coordination is at its best as you have to deal with

 

unexpected situations.

 

On the other hand, enthusiasm is not enough when your knuckles are bleeding while you are hanging

 

at 150 meters from the ground and you still have 35 more meters to reach the top of the rocky mountain.

 

Your concentration and self-confidence play an important role in these types of activities and you can

 

think there is an incongruence when you have to remain calm and think fast at the same time.

 

Whether you practice one of these activities alone or in teams, don’t lose the focus and practice your energy

 

management so that your body has plenty of energy during the whole activity. And remember, you should

 

always be in touch with someone who knows where you are, this is the basic when practicing high risk sports.
